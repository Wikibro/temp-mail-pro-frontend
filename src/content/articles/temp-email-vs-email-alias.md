## Two Tools, Different Jobs

People often treat **temporary email** and **email aliases** as the same thing, but they solve different privacy problems.

- A **temp email** is best for short-term access and low-trust signups.
- An **email alias** is better for accounts you may keep and manage over time.

The right choice depends on how long you expect the relationship with the website to last.

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | Temp Email | Email Alias |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Best for | One-time signups | Ongoing accounts |
| Lifespan | Short | Long-term |
| Inbox control | Temporary | Persistent |
| Recovery access | Weak | Strong |
| Privacy level | High | Medium to high |

## Choose Temp Email When

Temporary email is the better option if you want to:

- test a product quickly
- unlock a coupon or download
- avoid future newsletters
- keep an unknown site away from your real identity

This is why it works well for **free trials**, public wifi portals, and early-stage product evaluations.

## Choose an Alias When

An alias makes more sense if you need:

- password resets later
- invoices and receipts over time
- a reusable identity for one category of sites
- better control over forwarding and filtering

If you want the strongest version of this setup, a custom domain is even better. The setup process is explained in [Private Domains: Get Your Own Temporary Email](/blog/private-domains-temp-email).

## The Real Privacy Difference

Temp email reduces exposure by keeping your personal inbox fully out of the transaction. An alias still points back to an inbox you control, so it offers **organization and filtering**, but not the same level of isolation.

That makes temp email better for low-trust websites and aliases better for medium-trust websites.

## A Practical Rule

Use this simple rule when deciding:

1. If you might never return, use temp email.
2. If you expect follow-up emails, use an alias.
3. If you need full control and branding, use a private domain.

## Bottom Line

Neither option is universally better. Temporary email is the cleaner tool for short-lived privacy, while aliases are better for durable account management. The real win comes from choosing the right layer of identity for the specific signup.
